### Figure 4 difficulty note

- Main obstacle:
  - Figure 4 is not a monotone energy-cap scan like Figure 3.
  - For fixed `g`, ordinary and ground-state feasibility are only found in a very narrow window near the true ground-state energy.
  - A coarse scan in `E` misses that window completely.
- Numerical bottleneck:
  - `cvxpy` cone compilation dominates wall time.
  - `CLARABEL` / `CVXOPT` are fast but return a small systematic negative margin, typically around `10^-5 ~ 10^-4`, even when PSD residuals are already tiny.
  - `SCS` can certify more of these near-boundary points, but it is much slower, especially at small `g`.
- Practical consequence:
  - full left/right boundary refinement for every `g` is currently too slow for a dense `0..100` scan in this local setup.
